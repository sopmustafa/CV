Opening

My name is Sop Mustafa, a high school student currently studying at NGS School in Phnom Penh, Cambodia, with an expected graduation in 2027. From an early age, I have been drawn to the worlds of engineering and technology, not as just an academic subjects, but as a way of thinking, building, and creating.

I grew up asking questions: how do machines fly? How does code become real world action? How can simple components combine to solve meaningful problems? Those questions drove me to build, compete, study new languages, and give back to my community all while still in high school.

This cover letter reflects the experiences, challenges, and achievements that have defined my high school years and shaped the person I am becoming.

High School Experiences

FPV Drone Engineering

My most defining project has been building a first-person-view racing drone entirely from scratch, sourcing and assembling the frame, motors, electronic speed controllers, flight controller, and FPV camera. Configuring Betaflight firmware and tuning PID values to achieve stable flight increase my knowledge and understanding of aerodynamics, electronics, and embedded systems far beyond what any textbook alone could teach.

RC Car Build

Alongside drone work, I designed and built a RC car, increasing my understanding of motor drivers, servo control, and wireless communication. Writing custom C++ firmware for the microcontroller to handle steering and throttle gave me practical experience with embedded programming and real-time hardware feedback loops.

Automatic Plant Watering System

I applied Python programming to solve a practical problem which is automating plant care. Using a soil moisture sensor and a microcontroller, I wrote a script that reads moisture levels and triggers a pump when the soil becomes too dry. This introduced me to IoT concepts, sensor integration, and the satisfaction of building something with immediate, visible real-world impact.

Mobile Library — Walk Street Volunteer

Beyond technology, I believe in community responsibility. I volunteered at the Mobile Library on Walk Street in Phnom Penh, helping organise collections, assist community members, and contribute to a space that promotes reading and education. This experience increased my communication skills dearly and also reinforced the value of giving back.

ICT Club & Japanese Language Course

Participating in the ICT Club at NGS allowed me to collaborate with other people on technology projects and strengthen my computational thinking. Simultaneously, completing a Japanese language course expanded my multilingual profile, which already includes Khmer, English, and Arabic preparing me for a future in an internationally connected technology industry.

Closing Statement

High school has been more than a stage in my education, it has been the foundation of who I am as a builder, thinker, and contributor. Every project, competition, volunteer session, and language course studied has added a meaningful layer to who I am becoming.

I am deeply passionate about the intersection of drone engineering, embedded systems, and software development. My goal is to pursue a career at the forefront of technology and engineering and the experiences, discipline, and skills I have built in high school have equipped me with the right mindset to get there.
